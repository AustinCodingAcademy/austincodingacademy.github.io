THE RISE OF
THE DEVELOPER | DESIGNER

Today, every company is a tech company, and if they're not already, soon everyone will be working in tech in some form or another.

Designers are no different. Whether working in a large agency or completing the latest freelance contract, designers are frequently interacting with developers and staying up to date on the newest technologies.

To keep up with clients' needs, the developer/designer hybrid (see also: a unicorn) has become as invaluable part of any team.

WHY LEARN TO
CODE AS A DEVELOPER

Have your designs executed exactly the way you want them - By understanding how web development works, you'll be able to design wireframes that don't need to be "touched up" by the developer.

Save time; code your own designs - When the distance between designer and developer is zero, the time to project completion is significantly dimished.

Raise you value - Freelance or corporate, startup, or agency, no matter where you work, being able to design and code makes for a valuable pair of assets.
