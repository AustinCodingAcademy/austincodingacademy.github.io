Classes Designed to Move at the Pace of Life

Our classes are broken up into 3 tracks, each comprised of 3 levels. These classes are designed to get you from beginner to job ready in a sustainable and accessible manner, without having to quit your job or mortgage your future.

Each track, as you’ll see below is broken up into Intro, Intermediate and Advanced level courses. Each one of these courses is a 10 week session that meets twice a week in the evenings. These tracks are comprehensive and every class will have homework, projects, and assessments to ensure comprehension, however you won’t be on your own. We maintain a strict 10:1 student to teacher ratio, plenty of one-on-one time and office hours with instructors, as well as a vibrant online community of current students and alumni that are all engaged in helping each other succeed.

Step 1:
Intro

Our Intro level course is the starting point for all three of our tracks and designed for people who are starting with little or no experience coding. By the end of this level students can build functional and professional quality websites as well as basic JavaScript apps.

Step 2:
Intermediate

Intermediate is where the tracks split into either Full Stack JavaScript, Full Stack C#/.NET or Web Design. In this level the difficulty ramps up and students start building more complex apps if they are in a Full Stack track or start diving into UI/UX and iterative design if they are in Web Design.

Step 3:
Advanced

The final stage of each track, the Advanced class is where students complete their journey and have the technical skills to be considered job ready. In the Full Stack tracks, students build full stack production-ready applications, while in the Web Design track learn about site deployment, testing and the process of building their business as a web designer.

Step 4:
Job Search

Throughout the 3 levels students are invited to participate in various career services events, from resume and interview workshops to field trips and networking events at local tech companies. Once done with their program, students begin searching for and applying to the best organizations in Texas.

3 Tracks
The biggest reason students join {{ site.acronym }} is to make a powerful and lasting change in their career.

Some are looking to join a large and established organization, others are looking to jump in with a hot new startup, and still others are simply looking to pick up a new skill to add to their freelance arsenal. Regardless of where you’re going, we have a track that guides you along that path.

All tracks start with the Intro course, a class designed to give a strong set of fundamentals and actionable tools that are valuable regardless of where your path leads. From there students choose their path with the Intermediate level: Full Stack JavaScript, Full Stack, C#/.NET, or Web Design.

JavaScript Full Stack Web Development

This track is for people who want to build apps and get a job as a developer. The frameworks we teach in this track lend themselves to companies that support more open source technologies, and these companies tend to be a bit newer, though this is not a rule.

C#/.Net Full Stack Web Development

Similar to the Full Stack JS track, this track is also for people who want to build apps and get a job as a developer. The main difference is that this track focuses on the C#/.NET stack, which typically lends itself to larger, more established companies, though again, this is not a rule.

Web Design

This track is for people who want to design beautiful web experiences and get a job as a UX/UI designer or a freelance web designer. Students learn both design and web development skills as well as the capabilities to launch their own freelance business.
